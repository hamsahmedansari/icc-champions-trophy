#CT17 FINAL: Pak v Ind Innings Report

Fakhar ton muscles Pakistan to 338 for 4

Azhar, Babar and Hafeez too come to the party to leave profligate India facing stiff run-chase in bid to defend title.

Azhar, Babar and Hafeez too come to the party to leave profligate India facing stiff run-chase in bid to defend title.

India found out the difference on Sunday at The Oval as Fakhar smashed 114 off 106 balls to set up Pakistan’s imposing 338 for 4 in 50 overs.

Fakhar came into the big match with scores of 31, 50 and 57 in his only three One-Day Internationals and extended his form, becoming the first Pakistan batsman to score a ton in a final of an ICC event. It could so easily have been his first failure when he was caught behind on three, but Jasprit Bumrah had overstepped.

Everything apart from the toss went Pakistan’s way. Sent in to bat, Pakistan rode on Fakhar Zaman’s maiden ODI ton to post a daunting 338 for 4 before Mohammad Amir’s carnage with the ball destroyed India for 158 in just 30.3 overs. The margin could have been even bigger if not for Hardik Pandya’s late 43-ball 76.

Fakhar made use of the good fortune and went on to add 128 for the opening wicket with Azhar Ali (59). Babar Azam (46) and Mohammad Hafeez (57* off 37) capitalised on the platform with crucial knocks.

In fact, the first phase of the innings was a contest of which team was sloppier. Quite uncharacteristically, India won that battle, which meant Pakistan edged ahead in the actual game.

The biggest miss though came when Bumrah had Fakhar caught behind in the fourth over, only for the wicket to be denied by a no-ball. That summed up Bumrah’s and India’s performance; the pacer bowled with no control, spraying wides and half-volleys in a first spell of 3-0-24-0.

But it wasn’t even like Pakistan’s batsmen were in complete control. Fakhar in particular was all nerves and scored only 14 off his first 27 balls. Once that phase was over though, he played some terrific shots, especially against Ashwin who was clubbed through the on-side with disdain.

By then, Pakistan had coasted to 114 in 20 overs with India looking clueless and wrecked. It had already bowled as many as seven wides, got a wicket off a no-ball and missed at least three run-out chances.
